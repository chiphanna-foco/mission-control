# Workshop.TeddyBear.com - SaaS MVP Roadmap

**Project:** Workshop.TeddyBear.com (Young Writers Platform)  
**Owner:** Teddy (Author Brand)  
**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Created:** 2026-03-08  
**Status:** MVP Planning Phase  

---

## Executive Summary

**Workshop.TeddyBear.com** is a SaaS platform designed to empower young writers (ages 8-18) and their parents to write, publish, and celebrate their stories in a safe, supportive community environment.

**Core Vision:** Transform every child writer into a published author.

**MVP Target:** Launch in Q2 2026 with core writing + publishing features  
**Revenue Model:** Freemium with premium publishing and community features  
**Projected Users (Year 1):** 5,000-10,000 active young writers  

---

## Part 1: Target Users Analysis

### Primary User Segments

#### 1.1 Young Writers (Ages 8-18) — PRIMARY USERS
**Profile:** Children and teens who love storytelling and want to share their work

**Psychographics:**
- Creative and imaginative
- Seeking validation and community feedback
- Want to see their work in book form
- May be shy or introverted (appreciate safe space)
- Excited by milestone achievements (first story, 100 followers, published book)

**Behaviors:**
- Write on weekends or after school
- Use devices: tablets, laptops, phones
- Watch YouTube book recommendations
- Follow author TikToks and BookTok trends
- Share work with trusted friends/family

**Needs:**
1. **Creative tools** - Easy-to-use writing interface
2. **Inspiration & guidance** - Writing prompts, story starters
3. **Publishing pathway** - Path to seeing their story "in print"
4. **Feedback & community** - Safe peer reviews, encouragement
5. **Achievement recognition** - Badges, milestones, certificates

**Pain Points:**
- Writing feels intimidating or "too hard"
- No clear pathway to getting published
- Fear of judgment from peers
- No platform designed specifically for young writers
- Parents don't know how to encourage writing
- Limited access to youth writing communities

**Willingness to Pay:**
- Free tier: Very high adoption
- Paid tiers: 5-10% conversion (for publishing, premium features)

---

#### 1.2 Parents/Guardians (Ages 30-55) — SECONDARY USERS
**Profile:** Parents who want to encourage their child's creative development

**Psychographics:**
- Value education and creative development
- Want to foster confidence in their child
- Seek safe, moderated online spaces
- Interested in tangible keepsakes (books)
- Pride in child's accomplishments

**Behaviors:**
- Monitor their child's online activity
- Support hobbies and extracurriculars
- Willing to pay for quality educational tools
- Share child accomplishments with grandparents
- Follow parenting blogs and educational content

**Needs:**
1. **Safety & moderation** - Trust the platform
2. **Easy publishing** - Print physical books without hassle
3. **Progress visibility** - See child's writing development
4. **Parental controls** - Privacy and access management
5. **Keepsakes** - Quality books to keep and share

**Pain Points:**
- Hard to find writing communities for kids
- Unclear how to support child's writing
- Concerns about online safety
- Expensive to print children's books
- No clear "next steps" after child finishes story

**Willingness to Pay:**
- Publishing services: High (willing to pay $20-50 for printed book)
- Premium features: Medium ($5-15/month)
- Yearly subscriptions: Medium (value-based)

---

#### 1.3 Teachers/Educators (Ages 25-65) — TERTIARY USERS
**Profile:** Teachers and homeschool educators who want to motivate young writers

**Psychographics:**
- Passionate about creative writing education
- Seek engagement tools for classrooms
- Want to celebrate student work
- Value community and collaboration
- Interested in innovative pedagogy

**Behaviors:**
- Use classroom management platforms (Google Classroom, etc.)
- Assign creative writing projects
- Want to showcase student work
- Research educational tools
- Active in teacher communities

**Needs:**
1. **Classroom integration** - Easy to set up with students
2. **Assignment tools** - Create writing challenges/prompts
3. **Feedback mechanisms** - Streamlined peer review
4. **Publishing capability** - Celebrate student work
5. **Progress tracking** - See class writing development

**Pain Points:**
- Limited classroom time for creative writing
- Hard to motivate reluctant writers
- No good way to celebrate student work
- Grading writing is time-consuming
- No platform designed for classroom use

**Willingness to Pay:**
- School/classroom licenses: High (institutional budgets)
- Professional development: Medium
- Volume discounts: Value-based

## Part 2: Core Features Definition

### Feature Prioritization Matrix

**Scoring:** Must-have (MVP) | Should-have (Phase 1.5) | Nice-to-have (Phase 2+)

### 2.1 Writing Tools (MUST-HAVE)

#### Rich Text Editor
**Description:** Distraction-free writing interface optimized for young writers

**Features:**
- Clean, intuitive editor (not MS Word complexity)
- Real-time word/character count
- Auto-save (local + cloud)
- Dark/light mode
- Adjustable font size and spacing
- Mobile-responsive design

**Why:** Core product — if writers can't write comfortably, nothing else matters

**MVP Timeline:** Weeks 1-4 (Quill.js or TipTap)

---

#### Story Structure & Organization
**Description:** Tools to help young writers organize their thoughts

**Features:**
- Create multiple stories/projects
- Chapter organization
- Outline view
- Story metadata (title, summary, genre, age group)
- Cover image upload
- Folder/collection organization

**Why:** Helps users manage multiple works; essential for serious young writers

**MVP Timeline:** Weeks 2-5

---

#### Writing Prompts & Inspiration
**Description:** Daily prompts to overcome writer's block

**Features:**
- Library of 100+ writing prompts
- Daily featured prompt
- Prompts by genre (fantasy, mystery, realistic fiction, sci-fi, etc.)
- Prompts by difficulty (beginner, intermediate, advanced)
- Create custom prompts (for teachers/parents)
- Prompt-based writing challenges

**Why:** Drives engagement; reduces barrier to starting

**MVP Timeline:** Weeks 3-6 (+ content creation)

---

### 2.2 Community & Feedback (MUST-HAVE)

#### Peer Review System
**Description:** Safe, structured peer-to-peer feedback

**Features:**
- Request feedback on drafts
- Guided review prompts (2-3 questions, not open critique)
- Comment threads on text
- "Like" reactions
- Appreciation badges
- Anonymous review option (for sensitive writers)

**Why:** Core engagement driver; builds community; improves writing

**MVP Timeline:** Weeks 5-8

---

#### Writer Profiles & Following
**Description:** Build writer identity and community connections

**Features:**
- Public writer profiles (with privacy controls)
- Profile customization (avatar, bio, favorite genres)
- Follow other writers
- Activity feed (stories published, milestones reached)
- Writing stats (stories written, words, feedback received)
- Writer level/badges (Beginner, Storyteller, Author, etc.)

**Why:** Gamification, motivation, community building

**MVP Timeline:** Weeks 4-6

---

#### Community Feed & Discovery
**Description:** Help writers discover stories and connect

**Features:**
- Trending stories (most read, highest rated)
- New story feed
- Stories by genre/tag
- Stories by age group
- Featured author spotlight
- Follow recommendations

**Why:** Content discovery; keeps users engaged

**MVP Timeline:** Weeks 6-8

---

## Part 3: Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Model: Freemium

**Philosophy:** Free for writing and community. Pay for publishing and premium features.

---

### 3.1 Free Tier: "Young Writer" (Freemium Base)

**Price:** $0/month  
**Target:** Core user acquisition, engagement, community building  
**Conversion Goal:** 5-10% convert to paid tiers

**Included Features:**
- ✅ Unlimited story creation
- ✅ Rich text editor
- ✅ Story organization (up to 20 stories)
- ✅ Writing prompts (full library access)
- ✅ Peer review (give + receive feedback)
- ✅ Writer profile (public)
- ✅ Community feed access
- ✅ Follow other writers
- ✅ Digital publishing (PDF download)
- ✅ Parental controls (basic)
- ✅ Moderated community

**Limitations:**
- Cannot print books (print-on-demand disabled)
- Limited storage (500 MB per user)
- No advanced analytics
- Generic writer badge
- 2 writing prompts per week (vs. unlimited)

**Monetization Strategy:**
- Free tier drives user growth and engagement
- 20-30% should not be able to publish without paying
- Premium features unlock clear value

---

### 3.2 Paid Tier 1: "Published Author" ($4.99/month)

**Price:** $4.99/month (or $44.99/year = 25% discount)  
**Target:** Young writers ready to print their first book  
**Expected Conversion:** 3-5% of free tier

**What's New (vs. Free):**
- ✅ Print-on-demand publishing (unlimited books)
- ✅ Professional cover design templates (20+ designs)
- ✅ ISBN assignment
- ✅ Expanded storage (5 GB)
- ✅ Advanced writer analytics
- ✅ Premium writer badge
- ✅ Priority support
- ✅ Monthly writing challenges (with prizes)

**Not Included:**
- No AI writing assistance
- No classroom features
- No mentorship program

**Use Case:** A young writer who has written 2-3 stories and wants to print their first book(s).

---

### 3.3 Paid Tier 2: "Bestselling Author" ($9.99/month)

**Price:** $9.99/month (or $99.99/year = 17% discount)  
**Target:** Serious young writers, teachers, parent bulk purchases  
**Expected Conversion:** 2-3% of free tier

**What's New (vs. Published Author):**
- ✅ Everything in "Published Author"
- ✅ AI writing assistance (beta)
- ✅ Advanced analytics + reader insights
- ✅ Classroom integration (for teachers)
- ✅ Custom writing prompts (create own)
- ✅ Mentorship program access
- ✅ Writing workshops + webinars
- ✅ Featured author spotlight eligibility
- ✅ Custom domain for writer portfolio (teddybear.com/yourname)
- ✅ Batch book discounts (10% off print costs)

**Not Included:**
- No agency/publisher tools
- No professional editing service (can be added as add-on)

**Use Case:** Serious young writers (teens, prolific), teachers with classes, parents supporting multiple children's writing.

## Part 4: Technology Stack

### 4.1 Frontend

**Framework:** Next.js 14 (React)
- Fast, modern, great for SaaS
- Built-in optimization
- API routes for backend
- Excellent TypeScript support
- Great for both web and mobile-web

**UI/Styling:**
- Tailwind CSS (utility-first, rapid development)
- Shadcn/ui (pre-built components)
- Framer Motion (animations, delightful UX)

**Rich Text Editor:**
- TipTap (headless, customizable)
- Or: ProseMirror (more control, steeper learning curve)
- Rationale: Easy to extend with young writer features (word count, suggestions, etc.)

**State Management:**
- TanStack Query (React Query) — server state management
- Zustand — lightweight client state
- Rationale: Simpler than Redux, better for real-time features

**Real-time Collaboration (Phase 2):**
- Yjs + y-websocket (for multi-user editing)
- Plan: Phase 2, not MVP

---

### 4.2 Backend

**Framework:** Node.js with Express or Fastify
- Fast, JavaScript ecosystem (code sharing with frontend)
- Familiar to full-stack JavaScript developers
- Good library ecosystem

**API:** REST (MVP) → GraphQL (Phase 2)
- REST simpler for MVP
- GraphQL better for complex queries later

**Database:**
- **Primary:** PostgreSQL (relational, reliable)
  - User accounts
  - Stories & chapters
  - Comments & feedback
  - Profile data

- **Cache:** Redis
  - Session management
  - Feed caching
  - Real-time notifications
  - Rate limiting

**Search & Indexing:**
- Elasticsearch or Algolia (search stories by title, content, author)
- For MVP: PostgreSQL full-text search (simpler)
- Upgrade to Elasticsearch in Phase 1.5

---

### 4.3 File Storage & CDN

**File Storage:**
- AWS S3 (or Backblaze B2 for cost savings)
- Store: Story documents, profile images, cover images, PDFs

**CDN:**
- Cloudflare (global edge caching)
- CloudFront (AWS alternative)
- For fast downloads of PDFs and images globally

**Generate PDFs:**
- Puppeteer + headless Chrome (server-side rendering)
- Or: ReportLab (Python, if backend needs it)
- Or: Third-party service (Unidoc, PDFKit)

---

### 4.4 Authentication & Security

**Auth:**
- NextAuth.js (OAuth + JWT)
- Support: Email/password, Google OAuth, Apple Sign-In
- Parental consent verification (legal requirement for under-13 users)

**Security:**
- HTTPS everywhere (automatic with modern hosting)
- Rate limiting (to prevent abuse)
- CSRF protection
- XSS/SQL injection protection (built into modern frameworks)
- Parental consent verification (COPPA compliance)

**Compliance:**
- COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) — if under-13 users
- GDPR compliance (EU users)
- Age verification system

---

### 4.5 Print-on-Demand Integration

**Service:** PrintNinja or Lulu API
- PrintNinja: Higher quality, more author-friendly
- Lulu: Larger ecosystem, easier integration

**Integration Approach:**
1. Generate PDF from story + cover template
2. Send to PrintNinja/Lulu API
3. Receive order URL + pricing
4. User completes order
5. Webhook notification when book ships
6. Display "your book is ready!" notification

**Alternative:** Build direct integration with local printers (Phase 2)

---

### 4.6 Email & Notifications

**Email Service:**
- SendGrid or Postmark
- For: Welcome emails, password resets, notifications
- Template-based with dynamic content

**Push Notifications:**
- Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM)
- For: "New feedback on your story", "Story published", milestone achievements

**In-App Notifications:**
- Build custom with WebSockets (or use Firebase)
- Real-time feedback, follower notifications

---

### 4.7 Content Moderation

**Automated Filtering:**
- OpenAI Moderation API (detect inappropriate content)
- Or: Perspective API (Google, free)
- Flag suspicious content for human review

**Human Moderation:**
- Dashboard for moderation team
- Flag/review system
- Automated actions (warnings, content removal, account suspension)

---

### 4.8 Analytics & Monitoring

**Application Monitoring:**
- Sentry (error tracking)
- New Relic or DataDog (performance monitoring)

**Product Analytics:**
- Mixpanel or Amplitude
- Track: User onboarding flow, feature adoption, churn
- Identify: Which features drive engagement

**Logging:**
- ELK Stack (Elasticsearch, Logstash, Kibana) — open source
- Or: CloudWatch (if on AWS)

---

### 4.9 Deployment & Infrastructure

**Hosting:**
- Vercel (for Next.js frontend) — automatic deployments
- Heroku or Railway.app (for Node.js backend) — easy deployment
- Or: AWS EC2 + RDS (more control, more complex)

**Infrastructure as Code:**
- Terraform (manage cloud resources)
- Docker (containerize backend)

**CI/CD:**
- GitHub Actions (free, integrated with GitHub)
- Test, lint, deploy automatically on push
